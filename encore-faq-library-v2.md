## 1. Research Use Only

**What does "research use only" mean on this site?**
It means every product on this site is sold strictly for laboratory and institutional research — not for human or animal consumption, and not as a supplement, drug, or cosmetic.

**Are any Encore Bio Labs products approved for human or animal use?**
No. None of the products in this catalog are approved by the FDA or any other regulatory body for use in humans or animals.

**Is this catalog intended for personal use?**
No. This catalog is built for qualified researchers and institutions evaluating compounds for legitimate study purposes, not for personal wellness or self-administration.

**Does Encore Bio Labs provide medical advice of any kind?**
No. Nothing on this site is medical advice, a diagnosis, or a treatment recommendation, and our team does not offer medical guidance in response to questions.

**Why doesn't this site include dosing information?**
Because dosing and treatment protocols belong to a licensed medical context, and that's deliberately outside what this catalog does.

**Who is this catalog actually built for?**
Researchers, laboratories, and institutions with a real research question — not individuals looking for personal-use guidance.

**Is "research use only" a formality, or does it affect how the business operates?**
It affects real operating decisions: no dosing guidance anywhere on the site, research-use-only labeling on every order, and an intake process built around research context rather than checkout speed.

**What should I do if I have a health question this site can't answer?**
Speak with a licensed healthcare provider. That's a genuine recommendation, not a liability disclaimer.

Follow the format-specific handling guidance on that product's page, and treat all contents as research-use-only material requiring standard lab handling practices.

**What does the research-use-only labeling on my package actually mean?**
It confirms the contents are intended strictly for laboratory research, not for human or animal consumption.

## 6. Storage

**How should a product be stored once it arrives?**
Storage guidance varies by product and format — check the specific product's page for format-appropriate expectations.

**Do all products share the same storage requirements?**
No — storage can depend on format, lot, and documentation, which is why guidance is product-specific rather than one blanket rule.

**What can happen if a product is stored improperly?**
Improper storage can affect a compound's stability and usability in a research setting.

**Where can I find storage guidance for a specific product?**
Each product page includes storage-relevant context; more detailed documentation can be requested through intake.

**Does a reconstituted compound need to be stored differently than an unreconstituted one?**
Generally yes — reconstituted material typically has a different, often shorter, usable window than the original sealed form.

**Is refrigeration required for every product in the catalog?**
No — requirements vary by product, which is why we don't generalize across the catalog.

**Is there a standard shelf life across all products?**
No. Shelf life depends on the product, format, and lot — review product-specific documentation rather than assuming a universal timeline.

**Does the storage guidance on this site replace my own lab's protocols?**
No. It's educational context, not a substitute for your institution's own handling and storage protocols.

## 10. Product Questions

**Why do some products show more than one price?**
Because that product is available in more than one strength or format, each with its own price point.

**What's the difference between a "core" and "expanded" format?**
It typically reflects a difference in strength, quantity, or included components — the exact difference is listed on each product's page.

**Can Encore Bio Labs tell me which product is right for my research?**
We can help you compare research context and mechanisms, but we don't rank products or tell you which one is "best" for your specific study — that's a research-design decision for your team.

**Are product descriptions written for researchers or general readers?**
They're written to be readable without assuming prior expertise, while staying accurate enough to be useful to an actual researcher.

**Do product pages include published research citations?**
Where population-level published research exists for a compound, it's referenced as study-level context on that product's page.

**What does it mean when a product page says a compound is "studied for" something?**
It means the compound appears in research literature in connection with that biological pathway or question — not that the product itself produces that effect.

**Are any products on this site the same compound sold under two different names?**
A few compounds are associated with alternate names in research literature (for example, PT-141 and bremelanotide); product pages note this where relevant.

**Where do I ask a question about a specific product that isn't answered on its page?**
Reach out through the research intake process or WhatsApp with the specific product and question.
